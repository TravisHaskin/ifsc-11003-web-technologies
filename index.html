Progressive Enhancement Reflection

Progressive Enhancement is a perspective in web design in which a developer chooses to make the content of their work accessible to all users, regardless of browser, internet connection, etc. It primarily focuses on the content itself and implementing it into an accessible website. While the graceful degradation perspective focuses on prioritizing newer/more capable browsers and neglecting older/less capable ones, the progressive enhancement perspective does the opposite: it begins with ensuring that its content is accessible on the simpler/more dated browsers and then it makes changes and improvements as it reaches the more modern/complex browsers. In other words, it starts with the basic foundation and builds up.

The progressive enhancement is an important perspective to consider when designing a website. The internet is a vast place with many different users accessing it in various ways. Not everyone has access to the latest technology or the fastest internet connection, so it is crucial to ensure that the content of a website is accessible to as many people as possible. By prioritizing accessibility and usability, developers can create websites that are inclusive and user-friendly for all visitors.

Overall, the progressive enhancement is a valuable perspective in web design that prioritizes accessibility and usability for all users. By focusing on the content itself and ensuring that it is accessible to everyone, developers can create websites that are inclusive and user-friendly.
